Welcome to Jaden’s math blog!

I’ve been obsessed with probability ever since I took the intro course using Blizstein and Hwang’s Introduction to Probability. The book was filled with motivation and intuition, and I’ve had so much fun doing challenging problems and gaining deep understanding of the content. Therefore, I needed no convincing to jump to a measure-theoretic probability (MTP) course next using Billingsley’s Probability and Measure. However, my first impression of MTP is that it’s a huge leap from the intro course in terms of abstractness. I noticed that I often struggled to build intuition around the now formalized concepts that I used to be so familiar with in the intro course. This series of blog posts are my attempts at bridging my intuition gap between the intro course and MTP. If you have already taken an intro course, perhaps you will find it helpful too!
